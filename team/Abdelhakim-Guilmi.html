Abdelhakim GUILMI

Architecte & AAAG Founder - CEO

Biographie :
The Abdelhakim Guilmi - AAAG Achitecture Workshop was fouded by architect Abdelhakim GUILMI in 2005. Moroccan by origin and a native of Belgium, Abdelhakim Guilmi graduated with distinction from the Faculty of Architecture Lambert Lombard of the University of Liège in 2002 and then began a Masters in Landscape at the University of Granada in Spain. Which awards him his Masters with honors. Anxious to take part in the economic development experienced by Morocco, he created his own structure by offering work platforms abroad. It aims at the permanent exchange of knowledge and skills. Further accentuated by the occasional help of foreign partners and other design offices such as Ney+Partners, LVEP, etc. This synergy takes the impetus towards quality architecture without borders. Installed since its beginnings in the heart of Marrakech, the AAAG office operates both on national and international projects (UAE, Kuwait, Burkina Faso, Niger, Nigeria and Ivory Coast).


Professional Experiences :
• Restructuring of the WWA Group under the brand Atelier d'Architecture Abdelhakim Guilmi (AAAG) in 2011
• Establishment of the World Wide Architects (WWA) structure from 2005 to 2010, working on behalf of the architect Abdelhakim Guilmi.
• Architect - Project Manager at Associati agency in Marrakech from 2004 to 2005.

Research & Development :
• Tourism development project for the island of Corscio, Equatorial Guinea, 2009-2011.
• Residential and socio-economic development project for the village of Chichinya in Malabo, Equatorial Guinea, 2010-2011.
• Development project for an 18-hectare educational and sports center in Malabo, Equatorial Guinea, 2010-2011.
• Research and studies on specialized techniques for the restoration of the Marrakech city walls, Morocco, 2009-2010.

Professional development :
• Development of projects in Morocco and internationally.
• Establishment of the architecture agency 3AGE sl in Malabo, Equatorial Guinea, in 2011.

Languages :
French, Arabic, English, Spanish, and Berber fluently.
